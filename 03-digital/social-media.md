# Social Media Guide

## Status

Citi Maju Group's social media presence is in the early stage. Channels are to be established with care — quality over frequency. Do not post for the sake of posting.

---

## Platforms

**LinkedIn:** Primary platform. Target audience is property developers, procurement officers, engineers, and industry peers. This is where credibility is built.

**Facebook:** Secondary platform. Broader reach within the Malaysian market. Useful for general brand presence and potential recruitment.

**Instagram:** Lower priority for now. Consider only when a bank of quality visual content (site photography, project completions) is available.

Do not open a platform account without having a plan for consistent, quality content to populate it.

---

## Voice on Social Media

The social media voice is the same as the brand voice — direct, credible, precise — but with marginally more personality. LinkedIn in particular rewards content that shows genuine expertise and behind-the-scenes insight.

**Do:**
- Share project milestones with context (what the project involved, what the outcome was)
- Share certification updates, awards, or industry recognition
- Post about the technology or methods used (pipe jacking, hot tapping) — most people do not know what these are
- Acknowledge the team and workforce in progress updates
- Write captions in complete sentences

**Do not:**
- Post motivational quotes
- Use hashtag spam (5+ irrelevant hashtags)
- Share content that is not related to the business or industry
- Post half-finished or low-quality site photos
- Respond to negative comments publicly — take them to a direct message or email

---

## Content Types

### Project Update
Share at key milestones: mobilisation, major progress, completion.

**Template:**
```
[Project type] — [Location]

[One paragraph: what the project involves, the scale, any technical interest.]

[One sentence on the client relationship or project significance, without naming the client unless permission has been given.]

#WaterReticulation #PipeJacking #KlangValley #CitiMaju
```

### Certification / Credential Post
Use these to reinforce credibility. Keep it factual.

```
Citi Maju Engineering holds CIDB G7 Grade — Malaysia's highest contractor grade — alongside SPAN registration and Air Selangor Approved Contractor status.

These certifications mean our clients can procure with confidence, knowing we meet the full regulatory standard for large-scale water infrastructure work in Malaysia.

#CIDB #Infrastructure #KlangValley
```

### Team / Culture Post
Human, not performative. Show real work.

```
[Photo of team on site or in a project context]

Caption: [Name] and the team completing [task] at [project name]. [One sentence about what the work involved.]

Citi Maju Group — Klang Valley.
```

**Core set:**

**Technical when relevant:**
`#PipeJacking` `#DirectionalDrilling` `#HotTapping` `#CIDB` `#SPAN`

## Posting Frequency

| Platform | Target Frequency |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn | 1–2 times per week |
| Facebook | 1–2 times per week |
| Instagram | 2–4 times per month (when content is ready) |

Consistency matters more than frequency. A post every two weeks that is well-written and well-photographed is better than daily posts of mediocre quality.

---

## Photography Standards

All photos posted must meet minimum quality standards:
- Taken in good lighting (not dark, not overexposed)
- Horizontal framing preferred for most posts
- Site photos: equipment and work visible and clearly legible
- Team photos: staff in proper PPE and uniform where applicable
- No blurry, accidental, or poorly composed shots

No phone screenshots of documents. No photos of screens. No photos with strangers in the background who have not consented.

---

## Response Protocol

If someone contacts via social media DM with an inquiry, respond promptly and direct them to formal channels:

```
Thank you for reaching out. For project inquiries, please email us at inquiry@citimaju.com and we will follow up from there.
```

Do not conduct commercial discussions in social media DMs.
