# WhatsApp Communication Guide

## When to Use WhatsApp

WhatsApp is appropriate for:
- Quick operational updates with established contacts
- Scheduling confirmations and reminders
- Sending a document link to someone you have already corresponded with formally
- On-site coordination between team members

WhatsApp is not appropriate for:
- First contact with a new client or counterparty (use email)
- Sending quotations or formal offers for the first time
- Communicating anything that requires a paper trail (follow up with an email)
- Sharing sensitive commercial information without a prior formal email establishing context

---

## Tone in Professional WhatsApp

WhatsApp communication should be professional but proportionate. You do not need to write like a formal letter, but you should not write like a friend either.

**Do:**
- Use complete sentences
- Use proper capitalisation and punctuation
- Keep messages brief and to the point
- State who you are in the opening message of any new conversation

**Do not:**
- Use abbreviations like "pls", "tnx", "rgds", "FYI" (spell them out)
- Use excessive emoji
- Send voice notes to clients or counterparties without checking if they are acceptable
- Send multiple short messages in rapid succession — combine into one message
- Use ALL CAPS (it reads as shouting)

---

## Opening a New WhatsApp Conversation (Professional Contact)

Always introduce yourself in the first message if the contact may not have your number saved.

**Template:**
```
Good morning [Name],

This is [Your Name] from Citi Maju Group. [State the reason for reaching out in one sentence.]

[Your next question or action.]
```

Example:
```
Good morning Encik Farid,

This is Ivan from Citi Maju Group. I am following up on the site visit we scheduled for tomorrow at 9am.

Please confirm the access arrangements. We will arrive at the main guardhouse.
```

---

## Sending Documents via WhatsApp

Only send documents after formal correspondence has already been established by email. When sending:

- Name the document clearly in the message
- State what action is needed (review, sign and return, for reference only)
- Follow up by email if the document is important and no acknowledgement is received within a reasonable time

Example:
```
Hi [Name],

Sending over the quotation for your reference — this is the same document emailed to you earlier today.

Please acknowledge when you have received it.
```

---

## Group Chats — Project and Site Groups

For site and project WhatsApp groups:

- Name the group clearly: `CMG — [Project Name] — [Year]`
- Pin the project reference number, site contact, and start date at the top
- Keep discussion relevant to the project
- Use the group for operational updates, not for client-facing communication (clients should receive formal emails)
- When sending a critical update, confirm it in writing by email to the relevant parties

---

## Out-of-Office / After Hours

If you receive a WhatsApp message outside of working hours and cannot respond:
- Acknowledge receipt briefly if the message appears urgent
- Do not engage in extended discussion after hours unless genuinely necessary
- Set your WhatsApp status to reflect availability if needed

---

## What to Avoid

- Sending "noted" or "ok" as a standalone response — add the person's name and a full sentence
- Forwarding unverified news, articles, or chain messages in any Citi Maju group
- Discussing pricing, discounts, or contract terms over WhatsApp — use email
- Complaining about clients, projects, or colleagues in any group where external parties may be present
