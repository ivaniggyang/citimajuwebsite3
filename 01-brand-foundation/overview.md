# Brand Foundation — Overview

## What Citi Maju Stands For

Citi Maju Group is built on three things: technical competence, certified credibility, and consistent delivery. The brand exists to communicate all three, clearly and without embellishment.

This is not a brand that sells aspiration. It sells trust earned through track record — CIDB G7 certification, Air Selangor approval, years of large-scale infrastructure work across Klang Valley. The visual and verbal identity must reflect that.

---

## Brand Personality

**Authoritative, not arrogant.** We carry serious credentials. We state them clearly. We do not boast.

**Precise, not cold.** Our work is technical. Our communication is exact. But we are a Malaysian business with long-term client relationships — approachable when it matters.

**Restrained, not dull.** The visual identity is disciplined: navy, gold, clean type. Nothing decorative for its own sake. Every element earns its place.

---

## Aesthetic Direction: Industrial Refined

Think of the Goldman Sachs annual report. Steel and stone. The kind of brand that does not need to shout because its work speaks for itself. That is the register Citi Maju operates in.

- **Steel blue** is the dominant colour. It reads as technical authority and professional stability.
- **Refined gold** is the accent. Used sparingly — on the logo rule, on hairlines, on key highlights — to signal quality without decoration.
- **White space** is not wasted space. It signals confidence.
- **Typography** is structured and hierarchical. Cormorant Garamond for display, Sora for function, Noto Sans for body.

Materials that look generic, cheap, or like a template undermine everything the company has built. Every printed page, every email signature, every site hoarding is a touchpoint. Treat each one with care.

---

## Tagline

**"Trusted. Certified. Delivered."**

Use it:
- On namecards (back, as part of brand block)
- On site signage
- On the website footer
- In proposal cover pages

Do not:
- Modify it
- Shorten it
- Use it mid-sentence ("our team is trusted, certified, and delivered to your project")

---

## The Two Entities, One Group Rule

Citi Maju Group comprises Citi Maju Engineering Sdn Bhd and Citi Maju Construction Sdn Bhd. These are legal entities — not separate brands.

In all public-facing communication, they are **one group**. You may name the specific entity on formal documents (quotations, letterheads, contracts) where the contracting party must be legally clear. In all other contexts — website, presentations, namecards used for general networking, social media — the group is always presented as a whole.

Never write "our two companies" or "Citi Maju Engineering and Citi Maju Construction" in parallel as though listing two peers. The correct form is "Citi Maju Group" with entity context added only where necessary.
