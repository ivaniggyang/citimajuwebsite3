# Proposals Guide

## Proposals vs Quotations

A **quotation** is a priced document responding to a specific scope request. A **proposal** is a more comprehensive document that positions Citi Maju, articulates the solution, and builds confidence before or alongside pricing.

Proposals are used for:
- Major project tenders or open bids
- New client relationships where credibility needs to be established
- Presentations to property developers or GLCs where the decision is not purely on price
- Government procurement submissions

---

## Proposal Structure

A Citi Maju proposal has six standard sections. Do not omit any of them.

### 1. Cover Page
- CM Lockup (horizontal, Group or entity variant as appropriate) — centred or left-aligned
- Project name — Cormorant Garamond 400, large
- Prepared for: [Client name]
- Reference: [Proposal ref no.]
- Date
- Background: Navy (#071E3D) or Deep Navy with the CM mark watermark at low opacity

### 2. Company Overview (1 page)
Introduce the group. Always as one group. Include:
- What the group does (engineering and construction, with specific services)
- Territory: Klang Valley
- Certifications: CIDB G7 · SPAN Registered · Air Selangor Approved Contractor (for Engineering) — state them up front
- A brief note on track record without naming confidential clients unless permitted

Do not include: a company history timeline, a list of directors with full bios, generic mission/vision statements.

**Sample text:**
> Citi Maju Group is a Klang Valley construction and engineering group specialising in water reticulation, trenchless works, and renovation. Our Engineering entity holds CIDB G7 Grade — Malaysia's highest contractor certification — alongside SPAN registration and Air Selangor Approved Contractor status. We serve major property developers, government agencies, and GLCs across Klang Valley.

### 3. Understanding of Requirement (0.5–1 page)
Demonstrate that you have read the brief and understand the project. Restate the scope in your own words. Highlight any technical considerations or constraints you have identified.

This section builds confidence. A generic "we understand your requirements" is not acceptable.

### 4. Proposed Solution / Scope of Works (1–3 pages)
Detail the technical approach:
- Method of execution
- Key materials and standards
- Programme / timeline overview (if available)
- Subcontractors or specialist input (if any)

Use diagrams, section drawings, or photographs of similar works where available and relevant.

### 5. Credentials and Track Record (0.5–1 page)
State certifications again, with specifics:
- CIDB G7 — what this means (Malaysia's highest grade)
- SPAN registration
- Air Selangor Approved Contractor

Include 2–4 relevant project references in a table format:
| Project | Scope | Scale | Client Type | Year |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Name or description] | [Works type] | [RM value or m/km] | Developer / GLC / Government | [Year] |

Do not name clients without permission. "Major property developer, Shah Alam" is acceptable.

### 6. Pricing Summary / Commercial Terms
- Summary pricing table (detailed breakdown may be in a separate quotation document)
- Validity period
- Payment terms
- Exclusions

## Design Standards for Proposals

**Cover:** Navy background, white and gold type. CM lockup prominent.
**Interior pages:** White or Warm Paper background. Body text in Noto Sans. Section headings in Cormorant Garamond (H1/H2). Sub-headings in Sora.
**Tables:** Blue Pale header rows, alternating white/cream rows.
**Gold hairlines:** Separating header from body on each page. Between major sections.
**Page numbers:** Footer, Sora 300, right-aligned. Format: "Page X of Y"

**Do not use:**
- Stock photography of generic workers or construction sites — use real Citi Maju site photos where available
- Clip art or icon sets that look like a free template
- Multiple accent colours — navy, blue, and gold only

## Delivery

- Proposals are submitted as PDF — never editable Word documents
- File name: `CitiMaju-Proposal-[ClientName]-[YYYY-MM-DD].pdf`
- If submitted physically, print on 120gsm coated stock, saddle-stitched or spiral-bound depending on page count
- For tender submissions, follow the tender authority's submission format strictly — the proposal document sits within that format
