# Flyers and Posters

## When to Use

Flyers and posters are used for:
- Project awareness at a specific development or site
- Contractor/subcontractor recruitment
- Certification and capability announcements
- Events or industry engagements (when applicable)

Citi Maju does not produce promotional flyers in the consumer sense. All flyer and poster output should look like it comes from a serious engineering contractor — not a retail brand.

---

## Standard Sizes

| Format | Size | Common Use |
|---|---|---|
| A4 flyer | 210 × 297mm | Direct distribution, email attachment |
| A3 poster | 297 × 420mm | Notice boards, site offices |
| A2 poster | 420 × 594mm | Site hoardings, meeting rooms |
| Pull-up banner | 800 × 2000mm | Events, offices, reception areas |

All print files: 3mm bleed, 300dpi, CMYK.

---

## Layout Principles

**One clear message per piece.** Decide what the flyer must communicate before designing it. If there are three key points, rank them — the most important takes the most space.

**Hierarchy is non-negotiable:**
1. Main message — Cormorant Garamond 400, large, dominant
2. Supporting detail — Sora 500–600
3. Body copy or specifications — Noto Sans 400
4. Contact and logo — fixed position

**Margin:** Minimum 15mm on all sides (inside the bleed zone).

**White space:** Do not fill every centimetre. The page needs to breathe.

---

## Colour Usage in Flyers

| Background | Best for |
|---|---|
| Deep Navy (#071E3D) | Formal, high-impact announcements |
| White or Warm Paper | Information-heavy flyers with body copy |
| Blue Mid (#0D3362) | Secondary dark variant |

Do not use a full gold background. Do not use Blue Pale as the sole background for large-format print — it will look washed out.

Split layouts (half navy, half white) are effective for flyers that combine a bold visual message with detailed body text.

---

## Required Elements on Every Flyer

1. **CM Mark or Lockup** — top-left or top-centre, appropriate variant for the background
2. **Main headline** — Cormorant Garamond 400
3. **Entity or group identifier** — Sora 300, ALL CAPS, eyebrow label
4. **Contact details** — inquiry@citimaju.com + www.citimaju.com at minimum
5. **Tagline** (optional but preferred on external-facing pieces) — "Trusted. Certified. Delivered."

If the flyer represents the Engineering entity specifically (e.g., for a tender engagement), include the certification line: `CIDB G7 · SPAN Registered · Air Selangor Approved Contractor`

## Typography in Flyers

| Element | Font | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Eyebrow label | Sora 300, ALL CAPS | 9–11pt |
| Main headline | Cormorant Garamond 400 | 28–48pt (A4) |
| Subheading | Sora 600 | 13–16pt |
| Body copy | Noto Sans 400 | 9–10pt |
| Contact details | Sora 400 (labels) + Noto Sans 400 (values) | 8–9pt |

---

## Photography

Use real site photography wherever possible. It distinguishes Citi Maju from generic competitors.

- Preferred: site progress photos, completed project photos, equipment in action
- Acceptable: professional team photos in proper PPE
- Avoid: stock photography of workers or construction scenes not related to Citi Maju

Photography can be used as:
- Full bleed background (add navy colour overlay at 40–60% opacity so text reads clearly)
- Right-panel image (half-page, clean edge, no cut-out)
- Inset within a frame or border

---

## What Not to Do

- No WordArt, gradient fills, or drop shadows
- No clip art or free icon packs
- No more than two typeface families (Garamond + Sora, or Garamond + Noto Sans)
- No competing messages — one flyer, one purpose
- No promises of response time or guarantees not backed by fact
- Do not list machinery rental as a service

---

## Approval Process

All external-facing flyers and posters must be approved by the Director before print or distribution. Send a PDF proof for review. Do not print without written approval.

File format for print: PDF/X-1a or PDF/X-4 with embedded fonts, CMYK, 300dpi.
