# Voice and Tone — Overview

## How Citi Maju Sounds

Citi Maju Group communicates the way a senior engineer at a well-run firm would speak to a client: clearly, without fuss, with the confidence that comes from having done the work many times before.

The voice is consistent across every channel — emails, proposals, site signage, social media. The tone adjusts to context (a project update sounds different from a social media post), but the underlying character does not change.

---

## Brand Voice: Four Principles

### 1. Direct
Say what needs to be said. Do not pad sentences with qualifiers or filler. Do not bury the point in corporate language.

> **Write:** "We handle large-scale water reticulation projects across Klang Valley."
> **Not:** "We are pleased to offer our wide range of comprehensive solutions across various water-related project categories throughout the greater Klang Valley region."

### 2. Precise
Use the correct technical term. Do not simplify to the point of inaccuracy, and do not overcomplicate for its own sake. The audience for most materials is engineers, procurement officers, and property developers — they know the industry.

> **Write:** "Live main connections without service interruption." (hot tapping)
> **Not:** "We can connect to existing pipes while they are still running."

### 3. Credible
Let facts carry the weight. State credentials, certification numbers, and project scales where relevant. Do not use superlatives ("the best", "world-class") unless backed by a verifiable fact.

> **Write:** "CIDB G7 — Malaysia's highest contractor grade."
> **Not:** "We are one of Malaysia's most reputable construction companies."

### 4. Approachable
This is a Malaysian business built on relationships. The voice should not feel like a legal disclaimer. Write for a person, not a committee.

> **Write:** "If you have a project in mind, we are happy to discuss scope and feasibility."
> **Not:** "Inquiries may be directed to the relevant personnel for assessment."

---

## What the Voice Is Not

- **Not casual.** We are not informal, chatty, or colloquial in formal materials.
- **Not stiff.** We do not write in passive voice for the sake of sounding professional.
- **Not promotional.** We do not use hype, exclamation marks, or marketing hyperbole.
- **Not vague.** We do not hide behind abstractions when specifics are available.

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Proposals and tenders | Formal, precise, credential-forward |
| Client emails | Clear, professional, warm but efficient |
| Site instructions | Direct, unambiguous, no interpretation needed |
| Social media | Measured, informative, slightly more human |
| WhatsApp (professional) | Brief, clear, respectful |
| Namecard / signage | Concise, structured, zero decoration |

---

## Language

All primary materials are in English. Bahasa Malaysia will be added to select materials in a later phase. Until then, write in English only.

Do not mix languages mid-sentence. Do not use Manglish in any formal material.
