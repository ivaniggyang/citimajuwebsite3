# Writing Style Guide

## Sentence Structure

Write in active voice wherever possible. The subject does the action.

> **Active:** "We completed the pipe jacking works in February."
> **Passive:** "The pipe jacking works were completed in February."

Passive voice is acceptable when the actor is genuinely unknown, irrelevant, or when active voice would be awkward in a legal or technical context.

Keep sentences under 25 words. If a sentence is running long, split it.

---

## Paragraphs

Keep paragraphs short — three to four sentences maximum in body copy. In emails and WhatsApp messages, shorter still.

Use one idea per paragraph. Do not pack multiple points into one block.

---

## Punctuation

**Full stops:** Use them. Do not omit full stops at the end of sentences in bullets or lists if the items are complete sentences.

**Commas:** Use the Oxford comma in lists of three or more items. "Engineering, construction, and infrastructure works."

**Hyphens vs en dashes:** Use a hyphen (-) for compound modifiers ("large-scale project"). Use an en dash (–) for ranges ("2018–2024") and mid-sentence breaks.

**Exclamation marks:** Do not use them in formal materials. Avoid them in social media unless the context genuinely warrants it.

**Ampersands:** Do not use "&" in running text. Write "and". The ampersand may appear in table cells or graphic elements where space is genuinely constrained.

---

## Capitalisation

**Headers:** Title case for H1 and H2. Sentence case for H3 and below.
> H1: "Water Reticulation Services"
> H3: "What we deliver"

**ALL CAPS:** Reserved for: eyebrow labels, Sora-weight navigational elements, and specific namecard / signage typographic uses. Do not use ALL CAPS for emphasis in body copy.

**Bold:** Use for emphasis within body copy — sparingly. If everything is bold, nothing is.

**Certifications:** Capitalise as proper nouns. "CIDB G7", "SPAN Registered", "Air Selangor Approved Contractor."

**Entity names:** Always the full registered name in legal documents. In prose, "Citi Maju Group" is acceptable for general reference. "Citi Maju Engineering" and "Citi Maju Construction" are acceptable in context.

---

## Numbers

Spell out one through nine. Use numerals from 10 onwards.

For large numbers: "RM 1.2 million" not "RM 1,200,000" in body copy (the long form is acceptable in tables and financial documents).

For percentages: use the symbol — "14%" not "14 percent".

For phone numbers: `+6012-379 6562` — include the country code and use a hyphen after the first seven digits.

For dates: `15 April 2025` — no ordinals, no commas, no slashes in formal materials. Slashes (`15/04/2025`) are acceptable in tables.

---

## Lists

Use bullet points for unordered items. Use numbered lists for sequential steps.

In bullets:
- Start each item with a capital letter
- Be consistent — if one item is a full sentence, all items should be full sentences
- No full stop after the last item unless items are full sentences (then all items get full stops)
- Keep items parallel in grammatical structure

---

## Hedging and Qualifiers

Avoid: "may", "might", "could potentially", "in some cases", "generally speaking", "as much as possible."

These words signal uncertainty. State what is true. If something is conditional, state the condition directly.

> **Write:** "Works are subject to site survey. Contact us to arrange an assessment."
> **Not:** "Works may vary depending on various site conditions and may require additional assessment in some cases."

---

## What to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "World-class" | State the specific credential |
| "Comprehensive solutions" | List the actual services |
| "We are pleased to inform you" | Just inform them |
| "Please do not hesitate to contact us" | "Contact us at inquiry@citimaju.com" |
| "As per" | "As stated in" or "following" |
| "Kindly" | "Please" |
| "KL and Selangor" | "Klang Valley" |
| "Our two companies" | "Citi Maju Group" |
| Any response time promise | Do not state one |
