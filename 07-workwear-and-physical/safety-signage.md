# Safety Signage

## Compliance First

Safety signage in Malaysia is governed by the Occupational Safety and Health Act 1994 (OSHA) and related regulations. All mandatory safety signs must comply with the prescribed standards — MS ISO 7010 (Safety Signs) and relevant Department of Safety and Health (DOSH) guidelines.

Brand consistency is applied within compliance requirements, not instead of them. Do not modify a regulatory safety sign to match the Citi Maju colour palette if doing so compromises the sign's meaning or required specification.

---

## Types of Safety Signs

There are four categories of safety sign, each with fixed colour and symbol conventions:

| Category | Shape | Background | Symbol | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Prohibition | Round | White | Red border + slash | No smoking, No unauthorised entry |
| Warning / Hazard | Triangle | Yellow | Black symbol | Electrical hazard, Falling objects |
| Mandatory | Round | Blue | White symbol | Wear helmet, Wear safety boots |
| Safe condition / Emergency | Square/Rectangle | Green | White symbol | First aid, Emergency exit |

These colours and shapes are fixed by standard. Do not alter them.

---

## Where Citi Maju Branding Is Applied

Branding is applied to:
- **Site entry notice boards** — which combine regulatory information with company identity
- **Site office signage** — directional signs, room labels, project boards
- **Custom informational signs** — not governed by ISO 7010 (e.g., "Construction in Progress", project name boards, visitor instructions)

On all three, apply Citi Maju branding to the designated brand areas only. Do not place the CM mark on a yellow hazard triangle, for example.

---

## Branded Site Entry Sign

This is the primary opportunity for brand-consistent safety signage. A well-designed entry sign communicates:

1. Project name and Citi Maju identity
2. Access restrictions
3. PPE requirements for entry

### Layout
**Top section (brand block):** Navy background, CM Lockup (white), entity name
**Middle section (project info):** White or cream background, project name and site contact in Text Dark
**Bottom section (PPE requirements):** Three to five ISO 7010-compliant mandatory symbols in a row, with text labels below each icon

Example mandatory symbols to include (select as applicable):
- M014 — Wear safety footwear
- M007 — Wear eye protection
- M010 — Wear safety helmet
- M008 — Wear high-visibility clothing (if required by site)

Symbols must be ISO 7010 compliant — blue circle, white pictogram. Source ISO-compliant vector files from DOSH or a licensed safety sign supplier.

## Materials and Durability

| Application | Material |
|---|---|
| Permanent or semi-permanent site signs | 1.5–2mm aluminium or ACM, UV-printed |
| Short-term site signs (< 3 months) | 5mm PVC foam board or corflute |
| High-impact areas (hoarding, fencing) | 1mm aluminium |

All exterior safety signs must be UV and weather resistant. Standard digital print on paper is not acceptable for exterior use.

---

## Maintenance

Inspect all site safety signs at the start of each week. Replace signs that are:
- Faded or unreadable
- Damaged, torn, or bent
- Missing from their designated position

A safety sign that cannot be clearly read is not a safety sign — it is a liability. Keep a small stock of the most commonly used signs at the site office for immediate replacement.
